Complaints against Navient continue to persist


Student loan issues in Maryland and Virginia have resulted in 3,563 complaints against Navient, out of 35,364 complaints nationwide. Navient is one of the largest student-loan servicers in the U.S., handling both federal and private student loans. Starting in 2017, the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, along with many state attorneys general, accused Navient of widespread misconduct, including misapplying borrowersâ€™ payments, steering struggling borrowers into forbearance, and misreporting to credit bureaus. A settlement was reached in 2022, with one of the requirements forcing Navient to cancel $1.7 billion in subprime private student loan debt for more than 66,000 borrowers. However, not all borrowers were eligible.

Complaints against Navient still remain to be filed, although in smaller quantities. When comparing Maryland and Virginia, complaints by year steadily rose from 2012 to 2016, and peaked in 2017. In 2018, complaints dropped significantly until 2021. From 2021 to 2025, complaints gradually rose and fell. Currently, a total of 47 complaints have been made, with 29 issued in New York and 18 issued in Maryland. Federal loans were the most filed student loan type from Maryland and Virginia through the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau. This is also the case throughout the U.S., with a total of 16,026 complaints being federal loans alone. Number of complaints in Maryland and Virinia were against having issues with Navient than struggling to pay student loan debt.
