Revisiting a Nightmare: Hurricane Katrina

What makes this hurricane terrifying?

Hurricane Katrina wrecked havoc the most among U.S. states: Louisiana, Mississippi, Florida and Alabama, in late August 2005. Huricane Katrina was responsible for over 1,000 casualties and $100 billion in damage. In New Orleans, 80% of the city was flooded. Although mandated evacuation orders were issued, around 100,000 to 150,000 people remained in New Orleans. One surivivor, Karen Nix, reported on by the AP News, remained in New Orleans during the hurricane caring for patients at Tulane Medical Center. She said working during the hurricane was horrible, especially as her disability, cerebral palsy, made it more difficult. According to the National Weather Service, the powerful storm remains as one of the most devastating natural disasters in U.S. history.
